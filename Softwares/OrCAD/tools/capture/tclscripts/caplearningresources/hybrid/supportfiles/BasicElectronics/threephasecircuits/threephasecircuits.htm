Three Phase Circuits

This section discusses the basics of 3-phase circuits and explains how to simulate simple 3-phase sources in OrCAD® Capture-PSpice®.
A 3-phase circuit carries three alternating currents (AC), which are of equal frequency but reach their maximum amplitudes at different times. An ideal, balanced 3-phase circuit carries ACs of equal amplitude displaced from each other by an angle of 120° in time. 

Figure 1. Three phase waveform Figure 2. positive sequence phasor and negative sequence phasor

The 3-phase system can be connected either in a star formation or in a delta formation. The remaining sections discuss these two connections.



Star connection

In a balanced 3-phase system, the common reference point (N) for the three phase-voltages is called a neutral connection. The voltage sources can be defined either between the phases or between the phases and the reference point. The three sources VAB, VBC, and VAC are between the phases and are termed line-to-line voltages or line voltages in short. The three sources VAN, VBN, and VCN are between the phases and the reference point and are termed line-to-neutral or phase voltages.

The load of a 3-phase circuit is said to be balanced if the load impedances in each of the three phases are the same in both magnitude and phase.

For a balanced star-connected load, the neutral current is always zero. The line currents and phase currents are equal in magnitude, and the line currents are in phase with the corresponding phase currents.

Impedance (Z):-The opposition of a circuit to current when a voltage is applied is called electrical impedance, or simply impedance. It is the ratio of voltage to current in an AC circuit. Impedance has a magnitude and a phase. The magnitude of impedance is the ratio of the voltage amplitude to the current amplitude and the phase of impedance is the phase by which the current leads or lags the voltage. Concept of Power

A purely resistive circuit with only resistance does not return any power to the source because it uses all the supplied power. A purely reactive circuit with only reactance returns all the power to the source because it does not use any of the supplied power. In a circuit with both resistance and reactance, resistance dissipates part of the supplied power and reactance returns part of the supplied power to the source. As a result, circuits with both resistive and reactive elements lead to the following power concepts:

Concept of Resonance: At a particular frequency, called resonant frequency, inductive reactance (XL) is exactly equal to capacitive reactance (XC) in a circuit for any combination of L and C. A circuit is at resonance when XL is exactly equal to XC. Each RLC circuit responds to resonant frequency differently than it does to any other frequency. Because of this, an RLC circuit has the ability to separate frequencies.
